Trigonometry Notes

1. What is Trigonometry?

Trigonometry is the study of the relationships between the sides and angles of triangles.

2. The Six Trigonometric Ratios

  • Sine (sin): sin(θ) = opposite/hypotenuse
  • Cosine (cos): cos(θ) = adjacent/hypotenuse
  • Tangent (tan): tan(θ) = opposite/adjacent
  • Cosecant (csc): csc(θ) = 1/sin(θ)
  • Secant (sec): sec(θ) = 1/cos(θ)
  • Cotangent (cot): cot(θ) = 1/tan(θ)

3. Right Triangle Trigonometry

  • The sum of the angles in a right triangle equals 180°
  • The hypotenuse is always opposite the right angle
  • Use Pythagorean Theorem to solve right triangles: a² + b² = c²

4. The Unit Circle

The unit circle is a circle of radius 1 centered at the origin (0, 0) on the Cartesian plane.

  • Sine and cosine are the y and x coordinates of a point on the unit circle.
  • The unit circle helps in finding trigonometric values for various angles.

5. Trigonometric Identities

  • Pythagorean Identity: sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
  • Angle Sum Formula: sin(A + B) = sinA cosB + cosA sinB
