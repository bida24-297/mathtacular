Calculus Notes

1. What is Calculus?

Calculus is the branch of mathematics that studies change — it includes differentiation and integration.

2. Limits

  • Describes the behavior of a function as it approaches a certain value
  • Notation: limx→a f(x)

3. Derivatives

  • Measure the rate of change of a function
  • f'(x) or dy/dx
  • Rules: Power Rule, Product Rule, Chain Rule
  • Example: d/dx (x²) = 2x

4. Integrals

  • Opposite of derivatives — used to calculate area under a curve
  • Notation: ∫f(x) dx
  • Definite vs. Indefinite integrals
  • Example: ∫x dx = (1/2)x² + C

5. Applications

  • Velocity and acceleration
  • Optimization problems
  • Area between curves
