Statistics Notes

1. What is Statistics?

Statistics is the study of collecting, analyzing, interpreting, and presenting data.

2. Types of Data

  • Quantitative: Numerical (e.g., height, weight)
  • Qualitative: Descriptive (e.g., color, type)

3. Measures of Central Tendency

  • Mean: Average value
  • Median: Middle value
  • Mode: Most frequent value

4. Measures of Spread

  • Range: Largest - Smallest value
  • Standard Deviation: Measures how spread out the numbers are
  • Variance: Average of the squared differences from the Mean

5. Data Visualization

  • Bar charts, pie charts, and histograms
  • Used to represent data visually for better understanding

6. Probability

Probability measures the likelihood of an event happening. It ranges from 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain).
